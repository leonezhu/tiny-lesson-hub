Quick Reference: Articles (a / an / the)

Lesson 2 · Article reference card

Decision Tree

  • Is the noun singular + countable? → No → the or ∅. Yes → continue.
  • Is it specific / known? → Yes → the. No → continue.
  • Next word: vowel sound? → Yes → an. No → a.
ArticleUseExamples
a Singular, countable, non-specific. Before consonant sound. a pen, a university, a car, a good engineer
an Singular, countable, non-specific. Before vowel sound. an email, an hour, an MBA, an FBI agent
the Specific, known, unique. Any noun type. the report, the sun, the Thames, the information
∅ General plural, uncountable in general, fixed expressions. dogs, music, go to work, in hospital
Sound, not spelling: an hour (h is silent), a university ("yoo" = consonant), an FBI ("ef" = vowel), a one-eyed cat ("wuh" = consonant).
Always "the" with: rivers (the Thames), oceans (the Pacific), mountain ranges (the Alps), plural geographical names (the Netherlands), newspapers (the Times), superlatives (the best).
Always zero article with: general plural (Cats are nice), uncountable in general (I love music), institutions for their purpose (go to school/work/prison), meals (have breakfast), days/months/seasons (on Monday, in July, in summer).
WrongRightWhy
I love the dogs I love dogs General plural → zero article
an information some information / a piece of information "Information" is uncountable
a hour an hour Silent "h" → vowel sound
I saw a film. A film was great. I saw a film. The film was great. Second mention → known → "the"
