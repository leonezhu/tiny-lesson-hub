Relative Clauses — Quick Reference

Quick reference card · See full lesson: Lesson 7

Four Relative Words

WordUseExample
who People (subject) The engineer who designed it left.
which Things (restrictive or non-restrictive) The report which I sent has errors.
that People or things (restrictive only) The solution that we found was simple.
whose Possession (people or things) The client whose account was hacked.

Comma Rule

No commas = restrictive (essential info) → use that (things) or who/that (people)
With commas = non-restrictive (extra info) → use which (things) or who (people). Never use "that" with commas.

Omission Rule

You can skip who/which/that only when it's the object of the relative clause:

Subject → CANNOT omit: "The woman who lives next door…" (who does the action)
Object → CAN omit: "The book (that) I read…" (the book receives the action)

Chinese Speaker Pitfalls

1. No equivalent word: Chinese uses 的 before nouns for possession ("那个女人的房子"), but English requires whose: "the woman whose house."

2. No comma distinction: Chinese doesn't distinguish restrictive vs non-restrictive with commas. Remember: commas = extra info = use which, never that.

3. Omission confusion: Chinese freely drops pronouns. English only allows dropping relative words when they're objects, not subjects.
