Prepositions Quick Reference

at · on · in — same logic for time & place

The Specificity Funnel

Most Specific → at — points
Medium → on — days / surfaces
Most General → in — long periods / large areas

Time

PrepositionUse ForExamples
atSpecific clock times · momentsat 3pm, at noon, at midnight, at the moment
onDays · dates · named dayson Monday, on June 15th, on my birthday
inMonths · years · seasons · decadesin July, in 2025, in summer, in the 1990s
inParts of the dayin the morning, in the afternoon, in the evening

Place

PrepositionUse ForExamples
atSpecific points / locationsat the door, at home, at the bus stop, at reception
onSurfaces · streets · floorson the table, on Park Avenue, on floor 3
inEnclosed spaces · cities · countriesin Beijing, in the room, in my bag, in China

Exceptions to Memorize

at night — the one big exception to the "in the ___" rule. Say "at night" (no "the"), not "in the night".

on Monday morning — when you add a specific day to a part of the day, switch from "in" to "on": "on Monday morning", not "in Monday morning".

at Christmas / at Easter — for holiday periods as a point in time (vs "in the Christmas holidays" as a span).
