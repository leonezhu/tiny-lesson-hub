Email & Meeting English — Quick Reference

Lesson 15 · 参考卡 · Professional communication cheat sheet

Email — Politeness Ladder

  • Direct: "Send me the file." (rude)
  • Standard: "Could you send me the file?" (your go-to)
  • Extra polite: "Would you mind sending me the file?"
  • Hedged: "I was wondering if you could possibly..."

Email — Useful Phrases

  • Opening "Hope you're having a good week."
  • Follow up "Just following up on my email below..."
  • Request "Could you take a look at... when you get a chance?"
  • Pushback "I understand the urgency, but..."
  • Sign-off "Best regards," / "Best," / "Many thanks,"

Meeting — Starting

  • "Thanks everyone for joining. The purpose of today's meeting is..."
  • "Shall we get started?"

Meeting — Jumping In / Interrupting

  • "Could I just jump in here?"
  • "Sorry to interrupt, but..."

Meeting — Disagreeing Politely

  • "I see what you're saying, but..."
  • "I'm not sure I totally agree — have we considered...?"
  • "I'm afraid I disagree."

Meeting — Clarifying

  • "Just to make sure I've got this right — you're saying...?"
  • "Could you elaborate on that?"

Meeting — Wrapping Up

  • "So the action items are..."
  • "I'll follow up with an email recapping our decisions."
