Confusing Words — Quick Reference

Reference card for Lesson 14 · Confusing Words

核心规则： 记住词性比记住中文翻译更有用。同一个中文意思在英语中可能是不同的词，区别在于它是名词、动词还是形容词。

Affect vs. Effect

WordPart of SpeechChineseExample
affectverb影响The weather affected our plans.
effectnoun影响（结果）What was the effect?
effortnoun努力It took a lot of effort.

Trick: "The ______" = effect (noun). "The ______ the ______" = affect (verb).

Principal vs. Principle

WordPart of SpeechChineseExample
principaladj. / n.主要的 / 校长The principal reason. / The school principal.
principlenoun原则Act on principle.

Trick: "Principal" = your pal. "Principle" = rule.

Lie vs. Lay

WordTypeChineseExample
lieintransitive (no object)躺I lie down. / I lay down yesterday.
laytransitive (needs object)放下Please lay the book down. / She laid it down.

Trick: If something is being put down → lay/laid. If it's just "you" resting → lie/lay/lain.

Their / There / They're

WordMeaningChineseExample
theirpossessive他们的That's their car.
thereplace那里Put it over there.
they're"they are"他们是They're coming.

Trick: Can you say "they are"? → they're. Is it possession? → their. Is it a place? → there.
