Modal Verbs — Politeness & Hedging

Quick Reference Card · Full lesson: Lesson 5

Politeness Scale (same request, different levels)

  • Can you send me the report? — casual, peers
  • Could you send me the report? — standard polite
  • Would you mind sending me the report? — formal, very polite
  • May I ask a question? — formal permission

Hedging — Softening Claims

ModalStrengthExample
mayModerate"These results may indicate..."
mightWeak"This might be related to..."
couldModerate"This could lead to improvement."
shouldSuggestive"We should consider revising..."

Email Hedging: B1 vs B2

Direc
"I disagree with your approach.""I see your point, but I'm not sure this approach might be the most efficient."
"You need to fix this bug.""It looks like there might be an issue here that we should look into."
"Send me the data.""Could you possibly share the data when you get a chance?"

Key Rules

  • Could > Can for politeness. Always use could with clients/seniors.
  • Might < May for certainty. Might = slightly uncertain.
  • Hedging in writing is not weakness — it's intellectual honesty.
  • In meetings, should and might want to soften suggestions without commanding.
