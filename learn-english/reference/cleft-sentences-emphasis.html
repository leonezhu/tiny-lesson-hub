Cleft Sentences & Emphasis — Quick Reference

From Lesson 10 · B2 Level · English (B1→B2)

1. It-Cleft

It + be + [emphasized] + that/who + [rest]
EmphasizeExample
Person (subject)It was Sarah who wrote the report.
ObjectIt was the server that they fixed.
TimeIt was Wednesday that the meeting was.
PlaceIt was in production that we found the bug.
Negation contrastIt's not the budget — it's the timeline that's the problem.

2. What-Cleft (Pseudo-cleft)

What + clause + be + [emphasized]
TypeExample
ForwardWhat I need is a coffee.
Forward (explaining)What happened was that the server crashed.
ReverseA coffee is what I need.

3. Inversion Clefts

Only + fronted phrase → invert subject + auxiliary
PatternExample
Not only…Not only is the system reliable, but it's also fast.
Only after…Only after reading the logs did I understand.
Only when…Only when you test do you find real issues.

Key Rules

  • Who for people, that for things/time/place
  • No "which" in it-clefts — always "that"
  • No extra element at the end — the cleft has exactly two parts
  • Inversion: only + fronted phrase → auxiliary before subject
