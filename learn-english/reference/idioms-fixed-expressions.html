Idioms & Fixed Expressions — Quick Reference

10 idioms · B2 Level · From Lesson 13

Understanding & Agreement

1. Get the hang of it

To begin to understand or be able to do something after practice.

You'll get the hang of it after a few days.

2. See eye to eye

To agree completely with someone.

We see eye to eye on most project decisions.

3. On the same page

To have the same understanding or information.

Let's make sure we're all on the same page.

Action & Progress

4. Bite off more than you can chew

To take on more responsibility than you can handle.

You've bitten off more than you can chew with three projects.

5. Get the ball rolling

To start a process or activity.

Let's get the ball rolling with introductions.

6. Cut corners

To do something the easy/cheap way, sacrificing quality.

If we cut corners on testing, bugs will slip through.

Problems & Difficulties

7. Burn the midnight oil

To work late into the night, especially to meet a deadline.

We've been burning the midnight oil all week.

8. Back to the drawing board

To start over because the previous attempt failed.

The client rejected the design — back to the drawing board.

Communication & Information

9. Play devil's advocate

To argue the opposite side to test a position.

Let me play devil's advocate for a moment.

10. The bottom line

The most important factor; the final result.

The bottom line is that we need to ship by Friday.
