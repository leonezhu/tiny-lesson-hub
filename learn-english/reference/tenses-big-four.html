The Big 4 English Tenses

90% of daily English uses these four tenses. Master them first.

Tense Form When to use Example
Present Simple V / V+s Habits, facts, schedules I work from home. / She works remotely.
Present Continuous am/is/are + V-ing Happening now, near future plans I am working on a report. / We're meeting at 3.
Past Simple V-ed / irregular Completed actions in the past I finished the project yesterday. / She went to Berlin.
Present Perfect have/has + V-en Experience, recent changes, results now I have finished the report. / She's been to Tokyo.
Mandarin speakers, note: Chinese uses time words (昨天, 现在, 已经) instead of changing the verb. In English, the verb itself must change form. You can't say "I go to Shanghai yesterday" — the verb needs to be "went".

Present Simple vs Present Continuous

Present Simple (permanent / routine) Present Continuous (temporary / right now)
I work in tech. I 'm working from a cafe today.
She reads a lot. She 's reading a great book right now.
We don't use that tool. We 're not using it this week.

Past Simple vs Present Perfect

Past Simple (specific past time) Present Perfect (connected to now)
I sent the email yesterday. I have sent the email. (it's done now)
She lived in London in 2020. She has lived in London for 5 years. (still there)
Did you see the new feature? Have you seen the new feature?
Key signal words: Past Simple → yesterday, last week, in 2020, ago (specific time) Present Perfect → already, yet, just, ever, never, for, since (no specific time, or time continues to now)
