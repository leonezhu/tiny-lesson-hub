Discourse Markers & Linking Words

Lesson 11 · ~12 minutes · Advanced speaking & writing

By the end of this lesson, you'll be able to use discourse markers and linking words to make your English sound more natural, logical, and fluent — moving from choppy, basic sentences to connected, professional communication.

Why this matters

When Chinese speakers talk in English, they often string ideas together with simple "and" or just new sentences. This sounds choppy and basic. In professional English, linking words and discourse markers show how ideas relate to each other — contrast, addition, cause, sequence. They are one of the clearest signals of B2-level fluency.

Think of it this way: without linking words, your English is like a list of bullet points. With them, it becomes a coherent argument or story.

Category 1: Adding Information (补充)

Furthermore / Moreover / Also

Used to add a related point — especially in writing and formal speech.

  • The system is fast. Furthermore, it's very easy to use. (adds a positive point in formal context)
  • She has strong technical skills. Moreover, she understands the business side. (stronger than "also" — the second point is even more important)
  • We need more budget. We also need more people. (informal — "also" is more conversational)

In addition (to) / As well as

Similar to "furthermore" but slightly more flexible.

  • In addition to the meeting at 3pm, we have a call at 5.
  • He speaks fluent Mandarin, as well as good Japanese.
中文思维误区：中文常用"而且"、"还有"来连接，但英文中"and"用多了会显得单调。当你想说"而且"时，试着用 "Furthermore," "Additionally," 或 "In addition," 开头，后面加逗号，这样更正式、更专业。

Category 2: Contrast (转折)

However / Nevertheless / Nonetheless

Used to introduce a contrasting point — the most common in professional writing.

  • The project is on budget. However, it is two weeks behind schedule.
  • The data was incomplete. Nevertheless, we proceeded with the analysis. (stronger — despite the problem)

Although / Even though / While / Whereas

Used to connect two contrasting ideas in a single sentence.

  • Although we agreed on the price, we couldn't agree on the timeline.
  • While the North China office grew, the South China office declined. ("While" = contrast, not time here)
  • Whereas Chinese teams tend to prioritize speed, US teams tend to prioritize accuracy. (more formal contrast)

On the other hand / Then again

Used to show the other side of an argument — especially in discussions.

  • Remote work saves time. On the other hand, it reduces spontaneous collaboration.
  • We could hire locally. Then again, it might be better to outsource. (informal, spoken)
常见错误：不要用 "But" 在正式写作的句首。用 "However," 代替。比如：不要写 "But we disagree." 改为 "However, we disagree." 但口语中 "But" 完全没问题。

Category 3: Cause & Effect (因果)

Therefore / Thus / Consequently / As a result

All mean "so / therefore" — choose based on formality.

  • The client changed the requirements. Therefore, we need to revise the timeline. (standard formal)
  • Several key team members left. Consequently, the launch was delayed. (emphasis on the outcome)
  • We didn't get enough feedback. As a result, we made assumptions. (natural, slightly less formal)
  • The cost per unit dropped to ¥12, thus increasing margins by 15%. (very formal, often followed by -ing)

Because of / Due to / Owing to

All mean "因为" — used before a noun, not a full clause.

  • Due to bad weather, the flight was cancelled.
  • The delay was caused by a server outage. (alternative: "because of" — "Because of a server outage, the delay...")
中文思维误区：中文常说"因为...所以..."连用。英文中不能同时用 "Because" 和 "So/Therefore"：不要写 "Because it rained, therefore we stayed home." 正确写法是 "Because it rained, we stayed home." 或 "It rained; therefore, we stayed home."

Category 4: Sequencing & Structure (顺序与结构)

First (of all) / Second / Finally / Lastly

The most common sequencing words — used everywhere from meetings to reports.

  • First of all, we need to define the problem. Second, we'll explore solutions. Finally, we'll pick one and test it.

Meanwhile / In the meantime / Subsequently

Used to connect events happening at different times.

  • We're updating the website. Meanwhile, the marketing team is preparing the campaign.
  • The investigation ended in March. Subsequently, new policies were introduced. (more formal, "after that")

To sum up / In conclusion / Overall

Used to signal you're wrapping up.

  • In conclusion, I'd recommend we proceed with option B. (formal writing / presentations)
  • To sum up, we're over budget but on schedule. (meeting context)
  • Overall, the project was a success. (general assessment)

Category 5: Clarifying & Emphasizing (澄清与强调)

In fact / Actually / In other words / That is to say

Used to clarify, correct, or rephrase.

  • The budget was tight. In fact, it was below what we requested. (emphasizing a stronger point)
  • The feature isn't broken. Actually, it's working as designed. (polite correction)
  • The system is inefficient. In other words, it wastes time and money.

Importantly / Notably / Particularly

Used to highlight something worth paying attention to.

  • Importantly, all findings were consistent across regions.
  • The team performed well, particularly the design department.
口语高频词："Actually" 和 "I mean" 是最常用的口语标记词（discourse markers），但要注意：英语母语者用 "actually" 做礼貌纠正时语气很自然，但中文学习者可能显得语气不自然。在正式写作中尽量少用 "actually"，用 "In fact" 或 "In reality" 代替。

Quick guide: choosing the right linker

Informal vs. Formal

Not all linking words are created equal. Here's a quick reference:

  • So (informal) → Therefore (formal)
  • And also (informal) → Furthermore / Moreover (formal)
  • But (informal) → However (formal)
  • Like (informal) → Such as (formal)
  • Anyway (informal) → Regardless (formal)
  • So yeah (informal) → Thus (formal)

Practice: Choose the Right Linker

Each question gives you a context and a sentence with a blank. Pick the best linking word or discourse marker to fill it.

Q1
You're writing a formal report. You want to add another positive point about a product.
The interface is intuitive. ________, the price is competitive.
Q2
You're in a meeting discussing two options. You want to show contrast.
Option A is cheaper. ________, it has fewer features.
Q3
You're explaining a cause in your email.
The server went down. ________, we couldn't process orders all afternoon.
Q4
You're correcting someone's assumption politely.
I thought the meeting was at 3. ________ — it's at 3:30.
Q5
You're giving a presentation and about to summarize your key point.
________, I believe our strategy is sound, but we need to accelerate execution.
Q6
You want to rephrase a complex point more simply.
The ROI is below our threshold. ________, this deal isn't worth pursuing.
Q7
You're discussing the pros and cons of a decision.
Remote teams save office costs. ________, they can feel disconnected from company culture.
Q8
You want to emphasize a strong point in a formal email.
The data shows improvement. ________, user engagement has increased by 40%.
