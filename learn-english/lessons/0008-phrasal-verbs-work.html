Phrasal Verbs You Actually Need at Work

Lesson 8 · ~12 minutes · Workplace communication

By the end of this lesson, you'll recognize and correctly use 12 phrasal verbs that appear constantly in meetings, emails, and stand-ups — and know when they are separable (carry the report out vs. carry out the report).

Why this matters

In Chinese, you use a single verb: 提交, 提出, 推迟. In English, the same ideas are almost always phrasal verbs: submit vs. put in, 提出 vs. bring up. Phrasal verbs dominate spoken English at work — they are the difference between sounding natural and sounding like a textbook. Formal writing often prefers the single verb (investigate), but in conversation (meetings, chats, stand-ups) phrasal verbs are the default.

The Core 12

1. bring up — 提出（话题）

to mention a topic for discussion

  • Don't bring up the budget issue in the all-hands meeting.
  • How did she bring up the problem of delays?
  • I need to bring an important issue up with you.

Separable: You can say "bring up the topic" or "bring the topic up." With a pronoun: "bring it up" (NOT "bring up it").

2. follow up — 跟进

to check on something after it was first done

  • I'll follow up with the client tomorrow.
  • Can you follow up on that email from Tuesday?
  • We need to follow this lead up.

Follow up on = investigate/check. Follow up with = contact someone.

3. wrap up — 完成、总结

to finish or conclude something

  • Let's wrap up this call — we have 5 minutes.
  • We wrapped up the project ahead of schedule.
  • Can we wrap this up by EOD?

Separable: "wrap up the project" / "wrap it up." Very common in stand-up meetings: "I'll wrap up my tasks."

4. put off — 推迟

to delay something to a later time

  • We had to put off the launch because of the bug.
  • Don't put off until tomorrow what you can fix today.
  • The team put the decision off until next week.

Separable: "put off the meeting" / "put it off." Formal synonym: postpone.

5. run into — 遇到（问题/人）

to encounter a problem or person unexpectedly

  • We ran into some issues with the deployment.
  • I ran into Sarah from marketing at the elevator.
  • Watch out — you might run into resistance from the QA team.

Not separable. You say "run into a problem," never "run a problem into."

6. look into — 调查、研究

to investigate or examine something

  • I'll look into the error logs and get back to you.
  • The team is looking into alternative solutions.
  • Can you look into why the dashboard is loading slowly?

Not separable. "Look into the issue" — never "look the issue into."

7. set up — 设置、建立

to arrange or prepare something

  • Can you set up a meeting for Thursday?
  • I've set up the testing environment.
  • Let me set the conference call up in the calendar.

Separable: "set up a meeting" / "set it up." Very frequent in tech work.

8. figure out — 搞清楚、解决

to understand or solve something through thinking

  • We need to figure out who owns this ticket.
  • She figured out the root cause in ten minutes.
  • Let me figure this out before the stand-up.

Separable: "figure out the answer" / "figure it out." One of the most versatile phrasal verbs in English.

9. point out — 指出

to draw attention to something

  • As John pointed out in the review, the API is not backward-compatible.
  • Could you point out which test cases are failing?
  • I want to point this bug out to the team.

Separable: "point out the issue" / "point it out." Essential for code reviews and feedback.

10. cut down on — 减少

to reduce the amount of something

  • We need to cut down on meetings — we're spending 20 hours a week in calls.
  • I'm trying to cut down on context-switching.
  • We must cut the overhead down on this service.

Separable (with "down"): "cut down on meetings" / "cut the overhead down on..." Often used with "on."

11. keep up with — 跟上（进度/变化）

to stay at the same level as something changing

  • It's hard to keep up with the new framework updates.
  • Good — you're keeping up with the sprint.
  • Don't worry about keeping the docs up with every change.

Keep up = continue at same pace (not separable with "with"). The "with" introduces what you're following.

12. touch base — 联系、碰头

to briefly reconnect or update someone

  • Let's touch base after the client demo.
  • I'll touch base with design on the new mockups.
  • We should touch base every Monday morning.

NOT separable. Always "touch base (with someone) / touch base (on something)." Never "touch the base."

关键差异： 中文用一个动词搞定（"跟进"、"推迟"、"提出"），英语用"动词 + particle"组合。particle 不是介词——它是"状态转换操作符"。理解这一点，短语动词就不再是死记硬背的词表，而是有规律的系统。

Separable（可分离）：代词必须夹中间（carry it out, NOT carry out it）。
Not separable（不可分离）：宾语只能放在后面（run into a problem, NOT run a problem into）。
