Prepositions of Time & Place

Lesson 3 · ~10 minutes · Grammar foundation

By the end of this lesson, you'll know which preposition to use — in, on, or at — for time and place. Mastering this will eliminate one of the most common errors for Mandarin speakers.

The Golden Rule: Specificity

The same three prepositions are used for both time and place. The pattern is the same every time:

at = very specific  ·  on = more specific  ·  in = general / large

Think of it as a funnel — from specific to broad. This applies to both time and place. Once you internalize this, you won't need to memorize endless rules.

Why this is hard for Chinese speakers: Chinese uses different words for time and place (在点、在天的、在地的). English uses the same three prepositions for both — at, on, in — with the same specificity logic. Transfer the logic, not the words.

Prepositions of Time

AT — most specific points

Specific clock time · Specific moments

  • at 7:30am
  • at noon · at midnight · at sunrise
  • at the moment · at Christmas (the holiday period)
  • The meeting starts at 3pm.

ON — days and dates

Days of the week · Specific dates · Named days

  • on Monday · on my birthday · on New Year's Day
  • on June 15th · on Sunday morning
  • I'll see you on Friday.
  • The deadline is on March 1st.

IN — longer periods

Months · Years · Seasons · Decades · Parts of the day

  • in January · in 2025 · in the 1990s
  • in summer · in the morning/afternoon/evening
  • She was born in Chengdu.
  • We're going in October.
Common trap: in the morning / afternoon / evening — but on Monday morning. When a specific day is added, switch to on.

Prepositions of Place

AT — specific points / locations

A particular place on a map · A building as a point

  • at the bus stop · at the door · at reception
  • She's at work · I'm at home
  • Let's meet at the café on Main Street.

ON — surfaces · Lines · Floors

Things you can touch with your hand · Streets · Floors of a building

  • The book is on the table.
  • My office is on the 12th floor.
  • The house is on Park Avenue.
  • There's a coffee stain on my shirt!

IN — enclosed spaces · Large areas

Inside something · Cities · Countries · Rooms

  • The keys are in my bag.
  • He lives in Shanghai.
  • She's in the kitchen.
  • There are lots of trees in the garden.

Exception to memorize: at night (not "in the night" in normal usage). But say in the morning and in the evening. This is just one of those irregular things — treat it as a fixed phrase.

Practice: Choose the Right Preposition

Pick the correct preposition. There are 8 questions.

Q1
You're scheduling a meeting.
Let's meet ________ 3pm on Thursday.
Q2
Talking about when you started your job.
I started this job ________ January 2024.
Q3
Describing where someone lives.
She lives ________ Shanghai, China.
Q4
You're sending a file and it's late.
Sorry — the email is ________ my desk. Let me find it!
Q5
Inviting someone to dinner.
Can we grab dinner ________ Saturday?
Q6
Your friend is waiting for you.
I'm ________ the bus stop now. Where are you?
Q7
You're talking about the season.
It gets really hot ________ summer.
Q8
The important exception.
I'm not a night owl — I don't study ________ night.
