Collocations for Professional Writing

Lesson 12 · ~10 minutes · Business English vocabulary

By the end of this lesson, you'll be able to choose the right collocations for professional emails, reports, and meetings — moving beyond literal translations to sound natural and credible in English business communication.

What are collocations?

Collocations are word pairs that native speakers naturally use together. They're not random — they're patterns built by years of usage. Using the right collocation makes you sound fluent; using the wrong one makes your English sound "translated."

In Chinese, you might say "解决问题" (solve problem), which translates directly to "solve a problem" — that's fine. But "做决定" is make a decision, not do a decision. This kind of mismatch is the hallmark of non-native writing.

中文思维提醒：中文动词往往万能——"做决定"、"提建议"、"给帮助"。英语中每个名词都有固定的搭配动词。不要直译，要记固定组合。

Essential Collocations by Category

Decision & Action

Decisions, choices, and taking steps

Make a decision = 做决定

  • We need to make a decision by Friday.
  • It was a difficult decision, but we went ahead.

Take action = 采取行动

  • We need to take action before the deadline.
  • The government has finally taken action on climate change.

Take responsibility = 承担责任

  • She took responsibility for the delay and apologized.

Communication & Meetings

Emails, discussions, and collaboration

Raise an issue / bring up a point = 提出问题

  • I'd like to raise an issue about the project timeline.
  • He brought up a good point during the meeting.

Reach an agreement = 达成协议

  • We reached an agreement on the pricing model.

Provide feedback = 提供反馈

  • Could you provide some feedback on this draft?

Follow up on something = 跟进某事

  • I'll follow up on this with the engineering team.

Data & Analysis

Reports, metrics, and insights

Draw a conclusion = 得出结论

  • Based on the data, we can draw the conclusion that the campaign was successful.

Gain insight = 获得洞察

  • The survey helped us gain valuable insights into customer behavior.

Meet a deadline / meet expectations = 满足截止日期/期望

  • We're on track to meet the deadline.
  • The product met all our expectations.

Problem Solving

Addressing challenges and finding solutions

Resolve a conflict / resolve an issue = 解决冲突/问题

  • We've resolved the issue with the payment gateway.

Address a concern = 回应关切

  • Let me address your concern about security.

Implement a solution = 实施解决方案

  • We've implemented a new solution that reduces load times by 40%.
⚠ Common mistakes:
  • "Do a decision" → Wrong. Always "make a decision."
  • "Give advice" → Correct! But "give a suggestion" → Wrong. Say "make a suggestion."
  • "Commit a mistake" → Wrong. Say "make a mistake."
  • "Perform an effort" → Wrong. Say "make an effort."

Collocations that change meaning

Some collocations are so fixed that changing the verb completely changes the tone or meaning:

Strong vs. Weak Collocations

"Strongly recommend"Professional, confident
"Really recommend"Casual, spoken
"Conduct research"Formal, academic
"Do research"Informal, everyday
"Heavy rain""Natural English
""Big rain"Unnatural — sounds translated
""Fast food"Natural English
""Quick food"Unnatural
关键洞察：很多形容词搭配是任意的。英语说"heavy rain"不说"big rain"，说"fast food"不说"quick food"。这些没有逻辑，只能靠积累。阅读越多，直觉越强。

How to build your collocation bank

Don't memorize isolated words. Memorize chunks:

  1. When reading emails, highlight collocations — not individual words. Notice patterns: "reach an agreement," "meet a deadline."
  2. Keep a notebook (or digital doc) organized by category. Write the full collocation, not just the verb.
  3. Use them immediately. In your next email, consciously use 2–3 new collocations. Repetition cements them.
  4. Use a dictionary. Cambridge Dictionary and Oxford Learner's Dictionaries list common collocations for each word.

Why this matters

Native speakers don't think about collocations — they just feel right or wrong. At the B2 level, using natural collocations is what separates "good English" from "fluent English." Your ideas might be brilliant, but if you say "do a decision" instead of "make a decision," readers will doubt your professionalism.

Practice: Choose the Right Collocation

Pick the correct collocation for each professional scenario. There are 8 questions.

Q1
You need to tell your team about a choice you've decided on.
The team needs to ________ by end of day.
Q2
Writing a formal email to a client about resolving their complaint.
We have ________ regarding the billing discrepancy.
Q3
You want to politely bring up a concern in a meeting.
I'd like to ________ about the timeline — it seems unrealistic.
Q4
Your project is behind schedule and you need to act.
We can't just wait — we need to ________ immediately.
Q5
Describing what your research revealed about customers.
Our survey helped us ________ into purchasing behavior.
Q6
Writing a formal report conclusion based on data analysis.
Based on the quarterly figures, we can ________ that sales are recovering.
Q7
Describing the weather conditions affecting your outdoor event.
Unfortunately, ________ forced us to move the event indoors.
Q8
Following up after a meeting to confirm next steps.
I'll ________ the marketing team about the design changes.
