The Big 4 Tenses You Use Every Day

Lesson 1 · ~10 minutes · Grammar foundation

By the end of this lesson, you'll be able to choose the correct tense for the 4 most common situations in English — covering roughly 90% of what you say and write at work.

Why this matters

In Chinese, you show time with words like 昨天, 现在, 已经 — the verb never changes. In English, the verb itself must change. Getting this wrong is the #1 mistake Mandarin speakers make, even at B1 level. The good news: you really only need four tenses for most situations.

The Big 4

1. Present Simple — habits, facts, schedules

I/you/we/they work · he/she/it works

  • I work from home three days a week.
  • The meeting starts at 10.
  • She doesn't use that tool anymore.

2. Present Continuous — happening now, or near-future plans

I am working · she is working · they are working

  • I 'm working on the report right now.
  • We're meeting the client tomorrow. (future plan)
  • What are you doing?

3. Past Simple — completed actions at a specific time

I worked · she went · they finished

  • I sent the email yesterday.
  • We launched the feature last week.
  • She went to Berlin for a conference.

4. Present Perfect — experience, recent changes, result matters now

I have worked · she has gone · they have finished

  • I have finished the report. (it's done — result now)
  • She's been to Tokyo three times. (experience)
  • Have you seen the new design? (at any time up to now)
The hardest choice: Past Simple vs Present Perfect. Chinese uses 已经 + verb for both. In English: did you mention when? "I have finished the report" = no time mentioned, result matters now. "I finished the report yesterday" = specific time → Past Simple.

Quick check: signal words

These words tell you which tense to use:

Present Simple: always, usually, every day, often, sometimes
Present Continuous: now, right now, at the moment, currently
Past Simple: yesterday, last week, in 2020, two days ago
Present Perfect: already, yet, just, ever, never, for, since
