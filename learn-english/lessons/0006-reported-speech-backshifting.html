Reported Speech — Backshifting Made Simple

Lesson 6 · ~10 minutes · Intermediate grammar

By the end of this lesson, you'll be able to convert direct speech into reported speech correctly — including the backshifting of tenses, pronouns, and time expressions. You'll also know when NOT to backshift.

What is Reported Speech?

Direct speech repeats someone's exact words: "I work at Google." Reported (indirect) speech tells someone what another person said, without quoting them directly: She said she works at Google.

The main challenge for Chinese speakers: Chinese doesn't require tense changes in reported speech. 她说她在谷歌工作 — the verb "在" doesn't change. But English demands backshifting: moving the tense one step into the past.

The Golden Rule: Backshift

When the reporting verb is in the past (said, told me, explained), shift the original tense one step back:

Direct Speech Reported Speech
"I like this movie." She said she liked that movie.
"I am working on it." She said she was working on it.
"I finished the report." She said she had finished the report.
"I have finished." She said she had finished.
"I will call you." She said she would call me.
"I can help." She said she could help.
Key insight: Present Simple → Past Simple, Present Continuous → Past Continuous, Past Simple → Past Perfect, will → would, can → could. Think of it as moving everything one step further back in time.

Other Changes: Pronouns and Time Words

Backshifting isn't just about verbs. You also change pronouns and time expressions to match the new perspective:

Pronoun Shift

  • "I need my laptop." → She said she needed her laptop.
  • "We should ask our manager." → They said they should ask their manager.
  • "You should try your approach." → He told me I should try my approach.

When NOT to Backshift

Not every reported speech sentence needs backshifting. There are two key exceptions:

1. General truths and facts

If what was said is still true, backshifting is optional:

  • "The earth is round." → He said the earth is round. (still true — no backshift needed)
  • "Water boils at 100°C." → She explained that water boils at 100°C. (scientific fact)

2. Reporting verb in the present

If you use "says" instead of "said," no backshift:

  • She says she works at Google. (present reporting → no backshift)
  • He always says he will help. (habitual present → no backshift)
Common mistake for Chinese speakers: 中文不需要时态变化。你说"她说她在谷歌工作"，不管是什么时候说的，都是同一个句子。英文则要求根据上下文判断是否backshift。最安全的情况是：reporting verb是过去式（said）→ 默认backshift。

Reporting Questions and Commands

Reported speech isn't just for statements. Here's how to handle questions and commands:

Yes/No Questions

Use if or whether:

  • "Do you like the project?" → She asked if I liked the project.
  • "Are you coming tomorrow?" → He asked whether I was coming the next day.
  • "Can we reschedule?" → They asked if they could reschedule.

Wh- Questions

Keep the question word, but change word order (no "do/did" helper):

  • "Where do you work?" → He asked where I worked.
  • "What time did the meeting start?" → She asked what time the meeting had started.
  • "Why are you leaving early?" → He wondered why I was leaving early.

Commands & Requests

Use to + infinitive (not backshifted verb):

  • "Close the door." → She told me to close the door.
  • "Don't be late." → He told us not to be late.
  • "Please send me the file." → She asked me to send her the file.
